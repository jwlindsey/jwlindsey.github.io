The Journey

Most people pick a lane. I built a highway system.

My journey began with a simple refusal to say "I don't know how to do that." Instead, I replaced it with "I don't know how to do that yet." This mindset has taken me from the dusty floors of construction sites where I learned the logic of load-bearing walls, to the clean rooms of laboratories where I studied the microscopic engines of life.

I am an Arborist who understands the physics of leverage. I am an Investor who applies the patience of animal training to market volatility. I am a 3D Printer enthusiast who uses the precision of smithing to prototype new ideas.

Being a generalist isn't about lack of focus; it's about fluid adaptability. When a problem arises, I don't look for a hammer because I only have nails. I open a warehouse of tools and pick the perfect one.
